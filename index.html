Attractions

Perović (Arslanagić) bridge

The most famous monument from the Ottoman period in Trebinje and has a very important place in Balkan architecture of 16th century. Mehmed-Pasha Sokolović built the bridge 1574 in honor to his son who was killed in the battle with Venice.

Old Town

rebinje's Old Town, known as Kastel, is a charming historical district nestled along the banks of the Trebišnjica River. Built in the 18th century, its narrow stone streets, traditional Ottoman-style architecture, and well-preserved fortifications transport visitors back in time. It's a peaceful spot, perfect for strolling and soaking in the history and beauty of Trebinje.

City of Sun - Water and Dino park

Guests can indulge in various amenities, such as a spa, swimming pools, and sports facilities. The complex also features charming walking paths and stunning views of the surrounding hills, making it an ideal destination for families and couples seeking a peaceful retreat in nature. Religious landmarks

Hercegovačka Gračanica

A stunning Orthodox monastery located on Crkvina Hill overlooking Trebinje. Built in the traditional Serbian style, it is a replica of the famous Gračanica Monastery in Kosovo. The site offers breathtaking views of the city and surrounding landscapes, making it both a spiritual and scenic landmark.

Birth place of St Vasilije

The Birth House of Saint Vasilije, located in the village of Mrkonjići near Trebinje, is a modest yet deeply significant religious site. Saint Vasilije of Ostrog, one of the most revered saints in the Serbian Orthodox Church, was born here in 1610. The house, set amidst the peaceful rural landscape, has been preserved and restored, providing a glimpse into the saint’s humble beginnings. Nearby, a small church dedicated to Saint Vasilije has been built

Osman-paše Resulbegovića Mosque

The Osman-Paša Resulbegović Mosque is one of the most significant Islamic landmarks in Trebinje, located in the heart of the Old Town. Built in 1726 by Osman-Paša Resulbegović, the Ottoman governor of Herzegovina, the mosque is renowned for its elegant architecture and cultural importance. Its graceful minaret and dome stand out against the stone buildings of the town, while the interior features intricate Islamic decorations.

Duzi Monestary

The Duži Monastery, established in the 16th century, is a significant Serbian Orthodox site in Herzegovina. It was built during the Ottoman period, serving as a refuge for monks from Tvrdoš Monastery. The monastery has a rich history of religious significance and cultural preservation, standing as a testament to the resilience of the local Orthodox community amid foreign rule.

Tvrdos Monestary

Built on the foundations of a 4th-century church, it is one of the oldest cultural-historical monuments in the Republic of Srpska. Though destroyed during the Turkish-Venetian war, it was rebuilt, and today, visitors can see its glass floor revealing the original church foundations. The monastery also preserves a centuries-old winemaking tradition, maintaining new vineyards, a wine cellar, and a modern winery.

Church of Holy Transfiguration of Our Lord

This church has been honored many times throughout its existence, and one of the most important events was the arrival of St. Vasilije of Ostrog’s relics in 1996, whose headwear is still kept in the church. The church has a beautifully decorated garden with a monument honoring the recently deceased Serbian patriarch Pavle.

Cities near by

Dubrovnik

Dubrovnik is a city in southern Croatia fronting the Adriatic Sea. It's known for its distinctive Old Town, encircled with massive stone walls completed in the 16th century. Fun fact: Dubrovnik was the main filming location for King's Landing from Game of Thrones!

Distance: 33km (20 mi)

Budva

Budva is a town in Montenegro on the Adriatic Sea. Part of the Budva Riviera, it's known for sandy beaches and nightlife. Stone walls built by the Venetians surround the narrow streets of the medieval old town (Stari Grad)

Distance: 103km (64 mi)

Herceg Novi

Herceg Novi is a charming coastal town located at the entrance to the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro. Known for its rich history, picturesque old town, and stunning coastal views, the town boasts a unique blend of Mediterranean and Ottoman architecture. Visitors can explore its narrow, stone-paved streets, beautiful fortresses, and lush botanical gardens.

Distance: 40km (25 mi)

Mostar

Mostar is a city in southern Bosnia and Herzegovina, straddling the Neretva River. It’s known for the iconic Stari Most (Old Bridge), a reconstructed medieval arched bridge. The nearby alleys are full of shops and market stalls, and the Old Bridge Museum explores the bridge’s long history.

Distance: 118km (73 mi)
