Attractions

Perović (Arslanagić) bridge

The most famous monument from the Ottoman period in Trebinje and has a very important place in Balkan architecture of 16th century. Mehmed-Pasha Sokolović built the bridge 1574 in honor to his son who was killed in the battle with Venice.

Hercegovačka Gračanica

A stunning Orthodox monastery located on Crkvina Hill overlooking Trebinje. Built in the traditional Serbian style, it is a replica of the famous Gračanica Monastery in Kosovo. The site offers breathtaking views of the city and surrounding landscapes, making it both a spiritual and scenic landmark.

City of Sun - Water and Dino park

Guests can indulge in various amenities, such as a spa, swimming pools, and sports facilities. The complex also features charming walking paths and stunning views of the surrounding hills, making it an ideal destination for families and couples seeking a peaceful retreat in nature. Cities near by

Dubrovnik

Dubrovnik is a city in southern Croatia fronting the Adriatic Sea. It's known for its distinctive Old Town, encircled with massive stone walls completed in the 16th century. Fun fact: Dubrovnik was the main filming location for King's Landing from Game of Thrones!

Distance: 33km (20 mi)

Budva

Budva is a town in Montenegro on the Adriatic Sea. Part of the Budva Riviera, it's known for sandy beaches and nightlife. Stone walls built by the Venetians surround the narrow streets of the medieval old town (Stari Grad)

Distance: 103km (64 mi)

Herceg Novi

Herceg Novi is a charming coastal town located at the entrance to the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro. Known for its rich history, picturesque old town, and stunning coastal views, the town boasts a unique blend of Mediterranean and Ottoman architecture. Visitors can explore its narrow, stone-paved streets, beautiful fortresses, and lush botanical gardens.

Distance: 40km (25 mi)

Mostar

Mostar is a city in southern Bosnia and Herzegovina, straddling the Neretva River. It’s known for the iconic Stari Most (Old Bridge), a reconstructed medieval arched bridge. The nearby alleys are full of shops and market stalls, and the Old Bridge Museum explores the bridge’s long history.

Distance: 118km (73 mi)
